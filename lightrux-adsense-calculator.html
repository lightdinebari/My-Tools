Tips to Boost Your AdSense Earnings
📍 Target high-CPC niches. Finance, insurance, legal and tech content attract advertisers who pay more per click — often $1–$5+ CPC compared to $0.05–$0.20 for entertainment.
🌍 Drive traffic from Tier 1 countries. US, UK, Canada and Australian visitors generate significantly higher CPC than Nigerian or Indian traffic, even on the same content.
📐 Optimise ad placement. Ads placed within content (in-article) and above the fold consistently outperform sidebar or footer placements in both CTR and revenue.
⚡ Improve page speed. Faster pages reduce bounce rate, increase pages per session, and mean more ad impressions per visitor — directly boosting your RPM.

How to Use the AdSense Earnings Calculator

This free AdSense calculator helps bloggers and website owners estimate their Google AdSense revenue before or after monetisation. Simply enter your daily pageviews, your estimated CTR (click-through rate), and your average CPC (cost per click) to see projected earnings.

The calculator accounts for Google's 32% revenue share, leaving you with 68% of gross ad revenue. You can also set an income target and see exactly how many pageviews you need to reach it.

What is a good CTR for AdSense?
A typical AdSense CTR ranges between 0.5% and 3%. Most blogs average around 1–2%. Higher CTR usually comes from well-placed ads, relevant content, and engaged audiences. Above 10% can trigger a Google policy review.
What is RPM in AdSense?
RPM (Revenue Per Mille) is your estimated earnings for every 1,000 pageviews. It is calculated as: (Estimated Earnings ÷ Pageviews) × 1000. A typical RPM ranges from $0.50 to $5.00 depending on your niche and traffic source.
How much does AdSense pay per 1000 views in Nigeria?
For Nigerian traffic, AdSense RPM typically ranges from $0.10 to $0.80 per 1,000 views. Earning more requires targeting international audiences or high-CPC niches like finance and technology.
How many pageviews do I need to make $500/month?
It depends on your RPM. At an RPM of $1.00, you need 500,000 pageviews per month. At $2.00 RPM, you need 250,000. Use the calculator above with your target income to get your specific number.
Is this AdSense calculator accurate?
This calculator provides estimates based on the values you enter. Actual AdSense earnings vary based on advertiser competition, seasonality, content niche, and user geography. Use it as a planning guide, not a guarantee.

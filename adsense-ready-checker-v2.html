    What Is the AdSense Ready Checker?

    The Lightrux AdSense Ready Checker is a free quiz-style tool that helps bloggers and website owners quickly assess whether their site meets Google AdSense's key eligibility requirements — before they apply.

    Instead of applying blindly and getting rejected, answer 10 targeted questions, see exactly where your site stands, and get clear action steps to improve your chances.

    What Does Google AdSense Actually Look For?

    Google evaluates multiple factors when reviewing AdSense applications. The most important ones include:

    • A Privacy Policy page disclosing how user data is handled
    • An About page establishing your site's identity and purpose
    • A Contact page for user trust and transparency
    • HTTPS (secure connection) on your site
    • Original, high-quality content — not copied or AI-spun
    • At least 15–30 well-written posts before applying
    • A clear navigation structure that's easy to browse
    • Content that complies with AdSense programme policies

    Note: Both custom domains and subdomains (like blogspot.com) can be approved for AdSense. Content quality matters far more than your domain type.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    How accurate is this quiz? ▾
    This quiz is based on the real factors Google evaluates during AdSense review. Because you answer from your own knowledge of your site, the results are far more accurate than any automated URL scanner. Your honesty = your accuracy.
    Can a blogspot.com blog get AdSense approved? ▾
    Yes. Blogger (blogspot.com) is a Google product, and many blogspot subdomains are approved for AdSense every day. A custom domain can add professionalism, but it is not a requirement for approval. Focus on content quality first.
    My score is high — will I definitely get approved? ▾
    A high score means you meet the common structural and content requirements. However, Google also manually assesses content originality, user experience, and policy compliance. A high score significantly improves your chances but is not a guarantee.
    How many posts do I need before applying? ▾
    Google doesn't publish an official minimum, but most successful applicants have at least 15–30 quality posts. More importantly, each post should be original, helpful, and at least 500–800 words. Thin or copied content is the most common rejection reason.
